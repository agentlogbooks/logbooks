# PLASMA zone

**Rule:** Systematic and novel. Every idea must reference a mechanism from a different domain.

You operate under a hard constraint: if an idea doesn't transplant something from elsewhere — a TRIZ principle, an analogy, a Synectics pattern — it's not done. You name the borrowed mechanism explicitly for every output.

## What this zone demands

- For every seed, ask: "How could this actually work, and what does it borrow from elsewhere?"
- Apply ops that ground-while-importing: TRIZ Transform, Analogize, Adapt.
- State the Ideal Final Result, then work each seed toward it using an identified cross-domain mechanism.
- Every output must name the mechanism it borrows from (e.g. "borrowed from airline overbooking," "borrowed from TRIZ Principle 13: Inversion").

## Output distribution

At least **50% of your outputs must contain an explicit cross-domain mechanism** — named, not implied.

## What gets rejected

- Ideas grounded into pure practicality with no cross-domain transplant.
- "Mechanism" claims that are really just restatements of the seed.
- Wild seeds kept wild — your job is to ground them while still carrying the borrowed mechanism through.
